## Where It Shines (and Where It Doesn’t)

Wave’s free plan is the closest thing to a full accounting tool you’ll get without paying. The interface is clean, invoices look professional, and the mobile app lets you snap receipts on the go. But here’s the catch: Wave’s UK support is minimal. Their help docs are US-centric, and while the software technically supports UK VAT, you’ll need to manually configure it—no pre-built templates or HMRC-recognised formats. If you’re a freelancer or micro-business with simple needs, it’s workable. If you’re VAT-registered, prepare for extra admin.

Zoho Books’ free tier is more UK-friendly, with built-in VAT schemes and Making Tax Digital (MTD) compliance. The automation features—like recurring invoices and payment reminders—are surprisingly robust for a free plan. But the 1,000-invoice limit is a ticking time bomb. A small e-commerce business can hit that in three months. And while Zoho’s ecosystem is vast (CRM, inventory, etc.), the free plan locks you out of integrations like Shopify or PayPal unless you upgrade.

## The Rough Edges

Wave’s lack of UK-specific support isn’t just an inconvenience—it’s a compliance risk. Users on forums like UK Business Forums report having to manually adjust VAT settings for each invoice, which is error-prone. One user shared that they spent an entire weekend reconfiguring their setup after Wave’s default VAT calculations didn’t align with HMRC’s rules for partial exemptions. Zoho Books, meanwhile, has a steeper learning curve. The dashboard is cluttered, and the free plan’s single-user restriction means you can’t even grant your accountant read-only access without upgrading.

Both tools also share a glaring omission: payroll. Wave charges £20/month + £4 per employee for payroll, while Zoho outsources it to a third-party provider (starting at £5/month per employee). If you have even one employee, you’re looking at an extra £30–£60/month—enough to make a paid tool like FreeAgent (£19/month all-in) a better deal.

## What the Vendor Won’t Tell You

Wave’s free plan is free because they make money on payment processing. Their transaction fees (2.9% + 20p for card payments) are higher than Stripe’s (1.4% + 20p for UK cards), and they don’t offer volume discounts. If you process £10,000/month in payments, you’re paying £310 in fees with Wave vs. £160 with Stripe. Zoho Books, on the other hand, quietly restricts API access on the free plan. If you’re hoping to connect it to your e-commerce platform or CRM, you’ll need to upgrade to at least the £10/month plan.

Community sentiment is mixed. On Trustpilot, Wave scores 4.2/5 (as of June 2026), but UK users frequently complain about slow customer support and the lack of MTD-ready reports. Zoho Books fares slightly better at 4.4/5, but users gripe about the clunky mobile app and the fact that the free plan doesn’t include phone support.

## Who Should (and Shouldn’t) Use Free Accounting Software

**Use Wave’s free plan if:**
- You’re a freelancer or sole trader with fewer than 50 invoices/month.
- You don’t need VAT filing (or are happy to do it manually).
- You want a simple, no-frills tool and can live without bank feeds.

**Use Zoho Books’ free plan if:**
- You’re VAT-registered but have low invoice volume.
- You need MTD compliance and can tolerate the 1,000-invoice limit.
- You’re already using other Zoho tools (like CRM) and want to keep everything in one ecosystem.

**Avoid free plans if:**
- You have employees (payroll costs will eat into your savings).
- You process more than £5,000/month in payments (Wave’s fees will sting).
- You need multi-user access or integrations (Zoho’s free plan is too restrictive).

For everyone else, a paid tool like FreeAgent or QuickBooks Simple Start (both around £10–£20/month) is a better investment. The time you’ll save on VAT filing, bank reconciliation, and payroll will pay for itself—and you won’t wake up one day realising you’ve outgrown your "free" software.
