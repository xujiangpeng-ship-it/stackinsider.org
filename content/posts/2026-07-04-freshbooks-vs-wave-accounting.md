## What you’ll actually pay

FreshBooks has four plans. The Lite tier starts at $17 per month for five billable clients. The Plus tier at $30 per month lifts the client cap to 50 and adds recurring invoices and double-entry accounting. Premium at $55 per month removes the client limit entirely. Select, a custom plan, requires a sales call.

Wave offers a free tier that covers invoicing, accounting, and receipt scanning. The catch: payroll costs extra. In the US, payroll starts at $40 per month plus $6 per employee or contractor. In Canada, it’s $20 per month plus $6 per person. Payment processing fees apply separately: 2.9% plus $0.60 per credit card transaction, or 1% for bank payments with a $1 minimum.

Here’s how the costs stack up for a team of three with 30 clients and monthly payroll:

| Feature                | FreshBooks (Plus) | Wave (Free + Payroll) |
|------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------|
| Monthly base cost      | $30               | $58                   |
| Client limit           | 50                | None                  |
| Payroll                | Included          | $40 + $18             |
| Time tracking          | Included          | $16 (add-on)          |
| Payment processing     | 2.9% + $0.60      | 2.9% + $0.60          |
| Total first-year cost  | $360              | $796                  |

The free tier disappears fast once payroll and time tracking enter the picture.

## What sets each tool apart

FreshBooks centers on invoicing and time tracking. The interface lets you create an invoice in under a minute: pick a client, add line items from a saved list, set due dates, and send. Time tracking integrates directly; logged hours convert to billable line items with one click. The mobile app mirrors the desktop experience, including offline mode. That matters for teams that travel or work in spots with spotty Wi-Fi.

Wave focuses on accounting first. The dashboard shows cash flow, profit and loss, and balance sheet in a single view. Invoicing exists but feels secondary. The invoice editor lacks saved line items, so repetitive work takes longer. Time tracking is an add-on that costs $16 per month, and the mobile app only handles receipt scanning and basic expense entry. Offline mode is missing.

## Where each tool falls short

FreshBooks charges per client. If your client list grows past 50, the Premium tier at $55 per month kicks in. That’s steep for a freelancer who bills 100 clients a year. The reporting module is basic; custom reports require exporting to Excel. Payroll is US and Canada only, so international teams need a separate tool.

Wave’s free tier lacks payroll and time tracking. The payroll add-on only works in 14 US states and Canada. The mobile app can’t create invoices or log time. Customer support is email-only on the free plan; phone support costs $200 per year. Bank reconciliation is manual, which slows down monthly close.

## Who should pick which

Pick FreshBooks if you bill by the hour, need time tracking, or want payroll included. The client limit is the only hard cap; everything else scales with the plan. Teams that invoice 20-50 clients a month will hit the Plus tier sweet spot.

Pick Wave if you want free accounting and can live without time tracking or payroll. The free tier suits solopreneurs who invoice fewer than 10 clients a month and don’t need mobile invoicing. Once payroll or time tracking enters the picture, the cost jumps past FreshBooks.

Both tools integrate with Zapier, but FreshBooks has native connections to Gusto, Trello, and Asana. Wave only connects to Google Sheets and PayPal. If your workflow depends on other apps, FreshBooks reduces manual data entry.

G2 ratings as of June 2026 show FreshBooks at 4.5 stars from 1,200 reviews. Wave sits at 4.3 stars from 800 reviews. The gap widens in mobile ratings: FreshBooks scores 4.2 on iOS and 4.0 on Android; Wave scores 3.8 and 3.5.

The choice comes down to two questions. Do you need time tracking and payroll? Then FreshBooks. Do you want free accounting and can skip those features? Then Wave. Neither tool fits teams that need multi-currency support or advanced inventory tracking. For those, QuickBooks or Xero are better bets.
