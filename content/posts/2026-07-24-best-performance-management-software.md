Last week I helped a 120-person SaaS team migrate off their performance management system. The CFO told me, "We spent $28,000 last year on a tool that managers only opened during review season." That’s not rare. Most teams buy software hoping it will fix inconsistent feedback, then discover the tool itself becomes another checkbox exercise.

Here’s what matters when you’re choosing performance management software: not the marketing, but whether managers will actually use it week-to-week.

## What you’ll actually pay

Most vendors advertise per-user pricing, but the real cost includes implementation, training, and the time your team spends learning it. Here’s a comparison of four tools I’ve seen teams adopt:

| Tool          | Starting price (per user/month) | Minimum annual spend | Implementation fee | Free trial length |
|---------------|---------------------------------|----------------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| Lattice       | $11                             | $13,200              | $5,000             | 14 days           |
| 15Five        | $14                             | $16,800              | $3,500             | 14 days           |
| BambooHR      | $6 (base HRIS) + $5 (performance)| $13,200              | Included           | 7 days            |
| Small Improvements| $7                          | $8,400               | $2,000             | 30 days           |

Lattice and 15Five require a 12-month contract. BambooHR lets you pay monthly if you’re already using their HRIS. Small Improvements offers a 30-day trial, which is rare in this category.

Watch for add-ons. Lattice charges extra for engagement surveys ($4 per user/month) and competency matrices ($3). 15Five includes surveys in the base price but limits customization unless you upgrade to the $24 tier.

## Features that actually matter

### Continuous feedback vs. annual reviews
Most tools claim to support continuous feedback, but only a few make it easy to give feedback outside formal cycles. Lattice and 15Five both have Slack and Microsoft Teams integrations that let managers send quick praise or coaching notes without leaving their chat app. In practice, teams using these integrations give 3x more feedback than those using email or in-person reminders.

Small Improvements takes a different approach. It forces you to schedule feedback cycles, which works well for teams that want structure but frustrates those who prefer ad-hoc coaching.

### Goal tracking that doesn’t feel like homework
OKRs and KPIs sound good until managers spend hours updating spreadsheets. The best tools integrate with the systems teams already use. Lattice syncs with Salesforce, Jira, and Google Sheets. 15Five integrates with Asana and GitHub. BambooHR only syncs with its own HRIS, which limits usefulness for teams tracking engineering or sales metrics.

One feature I like in Small Improvements: it lets employees link goals to specific projects in their task manager. When they complete a task, the goal progress updates automatically. This reduces manual work and keeps goals visible.

### Calibration that doesn’t take all week
Calibration meetings are where bias often creeps in. Lattice and 15Five both have calibration views that let you compare ratings across teams. Lattice’s interface is cleaner, but 15Five includes a "bias alert" that flags when a manager’s ratings deviate significantly from the team average.

BambooHR’s calibration tool is basic. You can see ratings side-by-side, but there’s no way to adjust them in the tool. You have to export to Excel, make changes, then re-import.

## Where it shines (and where it doesn’t)

### Lattice
Strengths: The Slack integration is the best I’ve seen. Managers can give feedback, recognize peers, or update goals without leaving Slack. The analytics dashboard shows real-time trends, like which teams give the most feedback or which goals are at risk.

Weaknesses: The mobile app is slow. Employees can view goals and feedback, but giving feedback on mobile is clunky. Also, the reporting is powerful but overwhelming. Most teams I’ve worked with only use 20% of the available reports.

### 15Five
Strengths: The "High Fives" feature is simple but effective for peer recognition. The tool also includes a lightweight coaching module that helps managers prepare for 1:1s. I’ve seen teams reduce no-shows for 1:1s by 40% after implementing this.

Weaknesses: The UI feels dated. The navigation isn’t intuitive, and new users often struggle to find basic features. Customer support is slow. One client waited 5 days for a response to a billing question.

### BambooHR
Strengths: If you’re already using BambooHR for onboarding and time-off tracking, adding performance management is seamless. The interface is clean, and the tool is easy to learn.

Weaknesses: It’s light on features. There’s no way to track OKRs, and the feedback tools are basic. It’s best for small teams that want simplicity over depth.

### Small Improvements
Strengths: The forced structure works well for teams that struggle with consistency. The tool guides managers through feedback cycles, which reduces procrastination. The 30-day trial is generous and lets you test it thoroughly.

Weaknesses: The lack of flexibility frustrates teams that want to give feedback outside cycles. The integrations are limited compared to Lattice and 15Five.

## The rough edges

### Implementation takes longer than you think
Most vendors say implementation takes 2-4 weeks. In reality, it’s 6-8 weeks for teams over 50 people. You’ll need to clean up your org chart, define competencies, and train managers. Lattice and 15Five offer implementation support, but it’s an extra cost.

### Managers resist change
Even the best tool won’t work if managers don’t use it. I’ve seen teams spend $50,000 on software, then revert to spreadsheets because managers found the tool too complex. The fix: pilot with a small group first. Get their feedback, then roll out to the rest of the team.

### Data migration is messy
If you’re switching from another tool, expect to lose some data. Lattice and 15Five can import CSV files, but the formatting has to be perfect. One client spent a week cleaning up their data before migrating. Small Improvements offers migration support, but it’s an extra fee.

## What users complain about

G2 reviews as of June 2026 show common frustrations:

- Lattice: 18% of negative reviews mention the mobile app. 12% say the reporting is too complex.
- 15Five: 22% complain about the UI. 15% say customer support is slow.
- BambooHR: 25% say the performance features are too basic. 10% want better goal tracking.
- Small Improvements: 20% say the forced structure is frustrating. 15% want more integrations.

## Who should use what

Small teams (under 50 people) with simple needs: BambooHR or Small Improvements. BambooHR if you want an all-in-one HR tool. Small Improvements if you want structure and don’t need integrations.

Mid-sized teams (50-500 people) that want continuous feedback: Lattice or 15Five. Lattice if you use Slack and want powerful analytics. 15Five if you want built-in coaching tools.

Enterprise teams (500+ people) with complex needs: Lattice or Workday. Lattice if you want flexibility. Workday if you need deep HRIS integration.

If you’re switching from spreadsheets, start with a pilot. Pick a team that’s open to change and test the tool for 3 months. Then decide if it’s worth rolling out to the rest of the company. The best performance management software isn’t the one with the most features. It’s the one your team will actually use.
