Startups don’t need an HRIS that checks every box. They need one that doesn’t waste time, break the bank, or require a full-time admin to keep running. The problem? Most HRIS vendors pitch their software like it’s a Swiss Army knife, but in reality, you’ll use 20% of the features and fight the other 80% to do basic tasks. Here’s the truth about what actually works for early-stage teams.

## What You’ll Actually Pay (And Where the Gotchas Hide)

Most HRIS pricing pages show a clean per-employee-per-month (PEPM) rate, but the real cost includes setup fees, add-ons, and overage charges you won’t see until the invoice arrives. For example, **BambooHR’s “Essentials” plan starts at $5.25 PEPM** (as of June 2024), but that’s only if you commit to an annual contract and have at least 20 employees. Below that threshold, you’re looking at a flat $105/month—fine for a 10-person team, but a steep jump if you’re just starting with 5.

Rippling, another popular option, advertises a $8 PEPM base rate, but that’s for their “Core HR” module only. Add payroll, benefits administration, or IT provisioning, and you’re quickly north of $20 PEPM. **A 50-person startup I worked with last year budgeted $1,000/month for Rippling and ended up paying $1,800 after adding time tracking and device management.** The kicker? They didn’t even use the IT features—they just didn’t realize how quickly the add-ons stacked up.

Gusto, often recommended for its simplicity, has a $40/month base fee plus $6 PEPM. That’s $70/month for a 5-person team, which is reasonable, but if you need time tracking or advanced reporting, you’ll pay extra. **Gusto’s “Plus” plan, which includes time tracking, is $80/month + $12 PEPM**—suddenly, that $70/month budget balloons to $140 for the same team.

Here’s how the pricing shakes out for a 20-person startup, assuming you need payroll, basic time tracking, and benefits administration:

| HRIS          | Base Cost (Monthly) | PEPM Cost | Total (20 Employees) | Hidden Fees to Watch For                     |
|---------------|---------------------|-----------|----------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| BambooHR      | $105 (under 20 EE)  | $5.25     | $210                 | Setup fee ($500), add-ons like performance management ($2 PEPM) |
| Rippling      | $0                  | $8        | $160 (HR only)       | Payroll (+$8 PEPM), benefits admin (+$6 PEPM), IT features (+$8 PEPM) |
| Gusto         | $40                 | $6        | $160                 | Time tracking (+$6 PEPM), advanced reporting (+$6 PEPM) |
| Deel          | $49                 | $19       | $429                 | Contractor payments (+$49/month), global payroll (+$29 PEPM) |

Deel is the outlier here because it’s built for global teams, but if you’re a U.S.-only startup, the $19 PEPM price tag is hard to justify. **A founder I spoke with last month switched from Deel to Gusto after realizing they were paying nearly $500/month for features they didn’t need, like multi-currency payments and compliance docs for international hires.**

## Features That Actually Matter (And the Ones You’ll Ignore)

Startups care about three things: **hiring, paying people, and not getting sued.** Everything else is noise until you hit 50+ employees. Here’s what you’ll actually use—and what you can safely ignore.

### The Must-Haves
1. **Onboarding that doesn’t suck**
   The best HRIS for startups makes it easy to send offer letters, collect I-9s, and get new hires set up in payroll without a 10-step process. **BambooHR’s onboarding workflow is the gold standard here.** You can create a template for offer letters, set up automated reminders for missing documents, and even trigger a Slack message to the team when a new hire is added. Rippling does this well too, but with a clunkier interface.

   Gusto’s onboarding is fine for very small teams, but it lacks customization. You can’t, for example, add a step for collecting a W-9 from a contractor during onboarding—you have to do it manually later.

2. **Payroll that doesn’t require a PhD**
   If you’re running payroll manually in spreadsheets, stop. **Gusto’s payroll is the easiest to use for startups.** It handles federal, state, and local taxes, files W-2s, and even lets you pay contractors with a few clicks. BambooHR partners with TRAXPayroll for payroll, which is functional but not as polished. Rippling’s payroll is powerful but overkill for most startups—it’s more like a mini-ADP than a lightweight solution.

   One gotcha: **Gusto doesn’t support multi-state payroll in all plans.** If you have employees in multiple states, you’ll need to upgrade to the “Plus” plan ($80/month + $12 PEPM).

3. **Time tracking that doesn’t feel like a chore**
   Startups don’t need enterprise-grade time tracking, but they do need a way to log hours for hourly employees and track PTO. **Rippling’s time tracking is the most flexible.** Employees can clock in/out via mobile app, desktop, or even Slack. BambooHR’s time tracking is basic but gets the job done. Gusto’s time tracking is fine for simple use cases but lacks features like project-based tracking or overtime alerts.

   **Avoid:** Any HRIS that charges extra for time tracking. It’s a core feature, not an add-on.

### The Nice-to-Haves (That You’ll Probably Ignore)
- **Performance management:** Most startups don’t do formal reviews until they hit 50+ employees. If you’re using 15Five or Lattice for this, don’t pay extra for it in your HRIS.
- **Benefits administration:** If you’re using a broker like Justworks or Sequoia, you don’t need this in your HRIS. Gusto and Rippling offer benefits admin, but it’s often cheaper to keep it separate.
- **Advanced reporting:** Startups rarely need more than basic headcount and turnover reports. **BambooHR’s reporting is the most user-friendly**, but even then, most teams export data to Google Sheets for analysis.

### The Dealbreakers
1. **No mobile app (or a terrible one)**
   Your team will use the mobile app for two things: requesting PTO and clocking in/out. **Gusto’s mobile app is the best in class.** It’s fast, intuitive, and even lets you upload receipts for expense reimbursements. BambooHR’s app is functional but slow. Rippling’s app is buggy—employees frequently report issues with clocking in/out.

2. **Poor integrations**
   Your HRIS needs to play nice with your other tools. **Rippling has the best integrations**, with native connections to Slack, Google Workspace, and even IT tools like Okta. BambooHR integrates with 50+ apps, but many require Zapier or a custom API setup. Gusto’s integrations are limited—it works with QuickBooks and Xero, but that’s about it.

   **Hidden gotcha:** Some HRIS vendors charge extra for API access. Rippling, for example, charges $500/month for “Enterprise API” access if you need to build custom integrations.

3. **Terrible customer support**
   Startups can’t afford to wait days for a response when payroll is broken. **Gusto’s support is the most responsive**, with live chat and phone support during business hours. BambooHR’s support is solid but slower—expect a 24-hour response time for non-urgent issues. Rippling’s support is hit or miss. Some users report fast responses, while others say they’ve waited days for a resolution.

   **Pro tip:** Check the vendor’s status page before signing up. Rippling’s status page (status.rippling.com) shows frequent outages for payroll processing, which is a red flag if you’re running payroll weekly.

## The Rough Edges (What Users Actually Complain About)

No HRIS is perfect, and startups are particularly sensitive to friction. Here’s what users gripe about in forums and Slack communities:

1. **BambooHR: The “Essentials” plan is too basic**
   BambooHR’s “Essentials” plan ($5.25 PEPM) is marketed as a complete HRIS, but it lacks key features like time tracking, performance management, and advanced reporting. **A user on Reddit (r/startups, March 2024) complained that they had to upgrade to the “Advantage” plan ($8.75 PEPM) just to get PTO accrual tracking.** That’s a 66% price increase for a feature most startups consider table stakes.

2. **Rippling: Too many modules, too much complexity**
   Rippling’s modular approach is great in theory, but in practice, it’s overwhelming. **A CFO I worked with last year said their team spent 10+ hours setting up Rippling because they had to configure HR, payroll, benefits, and IT separately—even though they only needed HR and payroll.** The interface is also cluttered, with too many tabs and submenus.

   **Community sentiment:** On G2, Rippling has a 4.8/5 rating (as of June 2024), but many negative reviews mention “overwhelming complexity” and “steep learning curve.”

3. **Gusto: Limited customization**
   Gusto is the easiest HRIS to use, but it’s also the least flexible. **You can’t customize fields in onboarding, and the reporting is basic.** A founder in a Y Combinator Slack group complained that they had to export Gusto data to Google Sheets every month to generate custom reports for their board. BambooHR and Rippling offer more advanced reporting out of the box.

4. **Deel: Overkill for U.S.-only teams**
   Deel is built for global teams, but if you’re hiring only in the U.S., it’s overkill. **A user on Indie Hackers (April 2024) said they switched from Deel to Gusto after realizing they were paying $400/month for features they didn’t need, like multi-currency payments and international compliance docs.** Deel’s pricing is also confusing—you pay per contractor *and* per employee, which can get expensive fast.

## Where It Falls Short (And What to Use Instead)

No single HRIS is perfect for every startup. Here’s where each tool struggles—and what to use instead if it’s a dealbreaker:

| HRIS          | Where It Falls Short                          | Better Alternative If...                     |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| BambooHR      | No native payroll (uses TRAXPayroll)          | You need a single system for HR + payroll (Gusto or Rippling) |
| Rippling      | Too complex for small teams                   | You want simplicity (Gusto or BambooHR)       |
| Gusto         | Limited reporting and customization           | You need advanced reporting (BambooHR or Rippling) |
| Deel          | Overkill for U.S.-only teams                  | You’re hiring only in the U.S. (Gusto or BambooHR) |

**If you’re a 5-20 person startup with no HR admin:**
Use Gusto. It’s the easiest to set up, the payroll is foolproof, and the pricing is transparent. The lack of customization won’t matter until you’re bigger.

**If you’re a 20-50 person startup with a dedicated HR person:**
Use BambooHR. The onboarding and employee records are best in class, and the reporting is robust enough for most startups. Just be prepared to pay extra for payroll (via TRAXPayroll) or time tracking.

**If you’re a 50+ person startup with global hiring needs:**
Use Rippling or Deel. Rippling is better for U.S.-heavy teams with IT needs, while Deel is the clear winner for international hiring. Just budget for the complexity and higher cost.

**If you’re a founder who hates HR software:**
Use Gusto and outsource the rest. Pay a part-time HR consultant to handle compliance and benefits, and use Gusto for payroll and onboarding. You’ll save time and money in the long run.
