The real kicker? **Most teams overpay for features they don’t use**. HubSpot’s “omnichannel” marketing sounds great until you realize your team only needs email and SMS. Meanwhile, ActiveCampaign’s lower price tag comes with a steeper learning curve—users report needing 2-3 weeks of training to master its automation builder.

## Where It Falls Short (According to Real Users)

**1. Migration Is a Nightmare**
Moving from one CRM to another isn’t just about exporting CSV files. **Klaviyo users report losing historical email engagement data** when switching to HubSpot, and Shopify’s native CRM (yes, it exists) doesn’t let you export customer notes at all. The workaround? Manually copy-pasting or paying a third-party service like Cart2Cart $500+ to handle the migration.

**2. Support Is Hit or Miss**
G2 reviews from April 2026 show that **Omnisend’s support response time averages 4 hours**, while ActiveCampaign’s live chat often directs users to documentation instead of solving issues. The exception? HubSpot’s 24/7 phone support—but only on the Enterprise plan ($3,600/month).

**3. The “Free” Tier Is a Trap**
Most CRMs offer a free tier to lure you in, but the limitations are brutal. **Mailchimp’s free plan caps you at 500 contacts and 1,000 emails/month**, and you can’t even send abandoned cart emails. Klaviyo’s free tier is slightly better (250 contacts, 500 emails), but you’ll outgrow it in a month if you’re serious about ecommerce.

## The One Insight You Won’t Find on Vendor Websites

Here’s what no CRM’s marketing page will tell you: **The best ecommerce CRM isn’t always the one with the most features—it’s the one your team will actually use**. I’ve seen $50,000/year Salesforce implementations fail because the support team refused to log in, while a $300/month ActiveCampaign setup thrived because it integrated with their existing Slack workflows.

For example, a client of mine—a 7-figure Shopify store—switched from HubSpot to ActiveCampaign and saw a **30% increase in repeat purchases** not because ActiveCampaign was “better,” but because their team finally adopted it. The reason? ActiveCampaign’s automation builder is visual and intuitive, while HubSpot’s felt like coding.

## Which CRM Actually Fits Your Store?

- **If you’re a solopreneur or small store (under $500K/year):** Start with **Omnisend or Drip**. They’re affordable, easy to set up, and focus on the ecommerce essentials (abandoned carts, post-purchase emails). Skip HubSpot—you’ll outgrow it before you hit 10,000 contacts, and the migration headache isn’t worth it.

- **If you’re a mid-sized store ($500K–$5M/year) with complex workflows:** **ActiveCampaign or Klaviyo CRM** are your best bets. ActiveCampaign wins for automation flexibility, while Klaviyo is the king of email/SMS performance. Just budget for the learning curve—neither is plug-and-play.

- **If you’re enterprise ($5M+/year) and need deep customization:** **HubSpot Enterprise or Salesforce Commerce Cloud** are the only options that won’t buckle under your data volume. But hire a dedicated admin—these tools require constant maintenance.

- **If you’re on a tight budget and willing to DIY:** **Zoho CRM + Zapier** can work, but expect to spend weekends troubleshooting syncs. It’s the cheapest option, but you’ll pay in time, not dollars.

The bottom line? The “best” CRM for ecommerce isn’t about features or price—it’s about **which tool your team will actually adopt**. Test the free trials, but don’t just click around. Set up a real workflow—like an abandoned cart sequence or a post-purchase upsell—and see how long it takes. If it feels like a chore, keep looking.
