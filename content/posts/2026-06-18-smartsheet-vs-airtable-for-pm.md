## Alternatives to consider

If neither Smartsheet nor Airtable fits, these tools cover similar ground:

- **ClickUp**: Better for teams wanting everything in one platform. Less powerful than Airtable for database work. Starts at /user/month.
- **Notion**: Excellent for documentation-heavy teams. Lacks native Gantt charts. Free for small teams.
- **Monday.com**: Closer to Smartsheet in spreadsheet DNA but with stronger visual customization.

## Implementation timeline

Smartsheet typically takes 1-2 weeks to deploy for teams under 50 users. Import from Excel is straightforward. Training focuses on sheet templates and basic formulas.

Airtable requires 2-4 weeks for initial setup. The relational model demands more planning. Teams should map their data structure before building bases.

## Support quality

Smartsheet offers phone, email, and chat support on paid plans. Response times average 2-4 hours. Knowledge base is extensive with video tutorials and 15,000+ community members.

Airtable provides email and chat support. Phone support requires the Enterprise plan. Documentation is well-organized but less comprehensive than Smartsheet.

## Bottom line

Smartsheet wins for traditional project management with familiar spreadsheet interfaces. Airtable wins for teams needing flexible data structures. Choose based on your team comfort with spreadsheets versus databases.

## Which tool to choose

Pick Smartsheet if your team relies on spreadsheets and needs Gantt charts fast. It’s practical and easy to adopt. Choose Airtable if you manage complex workflows. Its relational model reduces manual updates for tasks like tracking features, bugs, and sprints in one place.

For teams under 50, Airtable’s flexibility outweighs its quirks. For enterprises with 200+ users, Smartsheet’s governance features justify the cost. Neither tool replaces a project manager, no matter how many AI features they add.
