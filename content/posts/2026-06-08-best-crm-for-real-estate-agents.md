Most real estate CRMs promise to "streamline your workflow" but start charging you the moment you try to do anything useful. Take Follow Up Boss, for example—its $69/user/month "Grow" plan locks transaction management behind the $129 "Scale" tier. If you’re closing more than a handful of deals a month, you’re already paying for features you assumed were included.

The real question isn’t which CRM has the most features—it’s which one won’t make you regret signing up after the first week.

## What Sets It Apart (and Where It Doesn’t)

### The Good: Features That Actually Save Time
**1. Automated Lead Routing (But Only If You Set It Up Right)**
Follow Up Boss and kvCORE both let you assign leads based on zip code, price range, or agent availability. The catch? kvCORE’s routing rules are buried in a settings menu most agents never find. Follow Up Boss does it better—its rules engine is front and center, but you’ll still need to manually adjust for edge cases (like a lead who’s already been contacted by another agent).

**2. Texting Without the Third-Party App Tax**
Most CRMs force you into a separate SMS tool (looking at you, BoomTown). Follow Up Boss and Real Geeks include native texting with templates, but Follow Up Boss’s interface is cleaner—no clunky pop-ups, just a thread that looks like iMessage. Real Geeks, on the other hand, limits you to 500 texts/month on its $299 plan unless you pay extra.

**3. Transaction Management That Doesn’t Feel Like a Spreadsheet**
Top Producer and kvCORE both handle contracts, deadlines, and task assignments, but Top Producer’s interface is outdated—think Windows 95 vibes. kvCORE’s transaction dashboard is modern, but it’s missing a critical feature: the ability to bulk-edit deadlines. If you’re managing 20+ deals at once, you’ll be clicking through each one individually.

### The Rough Edges: Where These CRMs Fall Short
**1. Mobile Apps Are an Afterthought**
Follow Up Boss’s mobile app lacks offline mode, which is a dealbreaker for agents who work in areas with spotty service. kvCORE’s app is better, but it crashes if you try to upload more than three photos at once. Real Geeks doesn’t even have a dedicated app—just a mobile-optimized website that’s slow to load.

**2. Integration Friction Is Real**
Most CRMs brag about their "100+ integrations," but the reality is messier. For example:
- **Follow Up Boss** integrates with Zillow, but the sync is one-way—leads flow into the CRM, but updates (like price changes) don’t push back to Zillow.
- **kvCORE** plays nice with Dotloop, but only if you’re on the $499/month "Broker" plan. Otherwise, you’re stuck exporting PDFs manually.
- **BoomTown** integrates with QuickBooks, but users report duplicate entries if you don’t map fields *exactly* right.

**3. Reporting Is Either Too Basic or Overwhelming**
Top Producer’s reports are so simplistic they’re almost useless—no custom dashboards, no way to track ROI by lead source. kvCORE’s reporting is powerful but overwhelming; its "Advanced Analytics" dashboard has 17 default widgets, most of which agents never use. Follow Up Boss strikes a balance, but its "Deal Flow" report doesn’t account for deals that fall through, skewing conversion rates.

## Where Each CRM Fits (And Where It Doesn’t)

### Solo Agents or Small Teams (1–3 Agents)
**Go with:** Follow Up Boss or Real Geeks
- **Why?** Both are affordable, include texting, and don’t require a PhD to set up. Follow Up Boss’s lead routing is a lifesaver if you’re juggling multiple sources (Zillow, Facebook, referrals). Real Geeks is cheaper but lacks transaction management—fine if you’re just tracking leads, not closing deals.
- **Avoid:** BoomTown. At $1,200/month, it’s overkill unless you’re generating 50+ leads/day.

### Mid-Sized Teams (4–10 Agents)
**Go with:** kvCORE or Top Producer
- **Why?** kvCORE’s transaction management and IDX integration are worth the $499/month if you’re closing 10+ deals/month. Top Producer is cheaper but clunkier—only choose it if you’re already using its MLS integration.
- **Avoid:** Real Geeks. The 500-text limit becomes a problem fast, and the lack of transaction tools will force you into a separate system.

### Brokerages or Large Teams (10+ Agents)
**Go with:** kvCORE or BoomTown
- **Why?** kvCORE’s "Broker" plan includes agent performance dashboards and commission tracking—critical for managing a large team. BoomTown’s lead gen tools are expensive but effective if you’re spending heavily on ads.
- **Avoid:** Follow Up Boss. The lack of transaction management on the base plan becomes a dealbreaker at scale.

## The One Thing No Vendor Will Tell You

Most real estate CRMs are built for *lead generation*, not *deal management*. If you’re spending 80% of your time on transactions (contracts, deadlines, compliance), you’ll hit walls with tools like Follow Up Boss or Real Geeks. kvCORE and Top Producer handle this better, but kvCORE’s onboarding is notoriously slow—users report it takes 2–4 weeks to fully migrate data and train agents.

**Pro Tip:** If you’re switching CRMs, budget *at least* 10 hours for data cleanup. Most agents underestimate how much junk accumulates in their old system (duplicate contacts, outdated listings, incomplete deal records). kvCORE’s support team will do a "free migration," but they won’t clean your data for you—that’s on you.

## Which One Should You Actually Choose?

If you’re a solo agent or small team, **Follow Up Boss** is the safest bet—it’s affordable, easy to use, and won’t nickel-and-dime you for basic features. Just be prepared to pay extra for transaction management if you scale up.

If you’re a mid-sized team or brokerage, **kvCORE** is the best all-in-one option, but the $1,000 onboarding fee and slow setup process mean it’s not a quick fix. Only choose it if you’re committed to using its transaction and IDX tools long-term.

For large teams with deep pockets, **BoomTown**’s lead gen tools are unmatched, but the pricing and integration headaches make it a non-starter for most. Stick with kvCORE unless you’re spending $5K+/month on ads.

The real "best" CRM? The one you’ll actually use. Most agents sign up for a CRM, log in twice, and never touch it again. Pick the tool that solves your *biggest daily frustration*—whether that’s lead routing, texting, or transaction tracking—and ignore the rest. The rest can wait.
