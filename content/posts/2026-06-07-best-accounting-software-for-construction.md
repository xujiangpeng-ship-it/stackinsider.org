QuickBooks is the cheapest upfront, but the per-user cost adds up fast. Sage is a middle ground, but the lack of offline mobile support will frustrate field crews. Foundation and Spectrum are pricier but include features that save time on job costing and equipment tracking. Viewpoint is the most expensive, but it’s the only tool here that handles everything from estimating to closeout in one system—if you can stomach the implementation fee and 3-year lock-in.

## Where It Falls Short

### The Community Complains About Support
On G2 (as of June 2026), Foundation Software has a 4.1-star rating, but the top complaint is support response time. Users report waiting 2–3 days for a callback, which is a problem if you’re trying to run payroll on a Friday. Sage 100 Contractor fares better at 4.3 stars, but users note that the support team often blames issues on “customizations” and charges extra for fixes.

### The Learning Curve Is Steeper Than Advertised
Most vendors claim you can be up and running in 30 days, but that’s only true if you’re replacing a simple QuickBooks setup. If you’re migrating from a legacy system like Timberline or Dexter + Chaney, expect 3–6 months of data cleanup, training, and parallel testing. Viewpoint’s implementation team is thorough, but they’ll charge you for every hour—budget $15,000–$20,000 for a mid-sized contractor.

### The “All-in-One” Promise Is Overhyped
Procore and Autodesk Construction Cloud market themselves as all-in-one solutions, but neither includes payroll. You’ll still need to integrate with ADP, Paychex, or a construction-specific payroll provider, which adds another $5–$10 per employee per month. If you’re running union jobs, you’ll also need a separate fringe-benefit tracking tool like UnionTrack or Fringe Benefit Management, which adds another layer of complexity.

## Who Should (and Shouldn’t) Use These Tools

If you’re a small residential contractor with 10 employees and 5 jobs at a time, QuickBooks Enterprise or Sage 100 Contractor will cover 80% of your needs without breaking the bank. The job-costing reports won’t be perfect, but they’ll be good enough to keep you out of trouble with your CPA.

If you’re a mid-sized GC running 15–30 jobs at once, Foundation or Spectrum will save you time on equipment tracking and change orders. The upfront cost is higher, but the time saved on reconciliations and payroll will pay for itself within a year.

If you’re a large commercial contractor with 100+ employees and complex union jobs, Viewpoint Vista is the only tool here that can handle the scale. The price tag is steep, but the alternative is stitching together 3–4 separate systems and dealing with the integration headaches.

Don’t buy any of these tools if you’re not willing to invest in training. The best accounting software in the world won’t help if your crew is still submitting paper timecards or your PMs are manually entering change orders into Excel. Start with one pain point—like job costing or payroll—and fix that before adding more modules. The rest can wait.
