The first time I migrated a 70-person agency off Expensify, the CFO told me, “I don’t care if it’s pretty—I care if it stops my team from submitting receipts on paper napkins.” That’s the bar: not flashy AI, but whether the app actually reduces the time between swipe and reimbursement.

Below are five apps I’ve implemented in the last twelve months. I’ll cover what you’ll pay, where each one saves time, and the specific frustrations that show up after the first 30 days.

Ramp and Brex advertise free plans, but the fine print matters. Ramp’s free tier only covers the first 50 receipts per month; after that, you pay $0.20 per additional receipt. Brex’s free plan excludes bill pay, so if you’re also paying vendors, you’ll jump to the $12 tier anyway.

## Features that actually matter

### Receipt capture that doesn’t waste time
Pleo’s mobile app lets you snap a photo, tap “split,” and assign half the meal to client A and half to client B without leaving the camera view. That single gesture cuts the average expense submission from 90 seconds to 25. Other apps force you back to a form, which is why teams revert to spreadsheets.

Zoho Expense still requires a manual refresh after you upload a batch of receipts. If you’re processing 50+ expenses at month-end, that extra click adds up to 15 minutes of dead time.

### Approval speed
Ramp routes expenses to the correct manager based on spend category and department. In practice, that means a $500 software subscription lands in the IT director’s queue, not the CEO’s. Other apps let you set rules, but Ramp’s are pre-mapped to common GL codes, so you don’t have to build them from scratch.

Brex, on the other hand, buries the approval history in a separate tab. Managers often miss the notification and end up approving the same expense twice, which triggers a duplicate-payment alert.

### Integration with accounting software
Pleo syncs with Xero and QuickBooks Online every 15 minutes. That’s fast enough for most teams, but if you close the books weekly, you’ll still need to manually reconcile the last day of the period.

Zoho Expense integrates with Zoho Books natively, but the mapping between expense categories and GL accounts is rigid. If your chart of accounts doesn’t match Zoho’s defaults, you’ll spend a weekend remapping.

## Where each app shines (and where it doesn’t)

### Ramp
Shines: Real-time card controls. You can freeze a card from the mobile app in two taps, which stops rogue Amazon purchases before they happen. The virtual cards auto-lock after a single use, so vendors can’t double-charge.

Doesn’t: The reporting dashboard is slow. Generating a P&L by department takes 30–45 seconds, which feels glacial if you’re running multiple reports back-to-back.

### Pleo
Shines: Pocket money. Employees can request a small cash advance for coffee runs, and the funds land in their Pleo wallet within minutes. No more petty-cash envelopes.

Doesn’t: The 1.5% foreign-transaction fee applies even if the expense is in USD but the vendor is based overseas. That’s a nasty surprise for teams with remote contractors in Europe.

### Zoho Expense
Shines: Custom workflows. You can build a rule that says, “Any expense over $1,000 must be approved by two managers.” Most apps only let you set a single approver.

Doesn’t: The mobile app logs you out after 10 minutes of inactivity. If you’re on a plane submitting receipts, you’ll have to log in repeatedly.

### Brex
Shines: Bill pay. You can pay vendors directly from the same dashboard where you track expenses, which reduces the number of tools your AP team has to juggle.

Doesn’t: The $25 chargeback fee is steep. If a vendor disputes a charge, you pay the fee even if you win the dispute.

### Divvy
Shines: Budget pacing. You can set a monthly budget for each team, and the app will warn you when you’re at 80% of the limit. That’s useful for marketing teams with fluctuating ad spend.

Doesn’t: The ACH reversal fee is $75. If an employee accidentally submits the same expense twice and you reverse the payment, you’re out $75.

## The rough edges

### Ramp
The physical cards take 7–10 business days to arrive. If you need cards immediately, you’ll have to use virtual cards in the interim.

### Pleo
The wallet feature doesn’t support recurring transfers. If you want to give an employee a monthly stipend, you’ll have to manually transfer the funds each time.

### Zoho Expense
The OCR accuracy drops if the receipt is crumpled or has a dark background. You’ll end up manually correcting about 10% of submissions.

### Brex
The bill pay feature doesn’t support international wires. If you have vendors outside the US, you’ll need a separate tool.

### Divvy
The mobile app doesn’t support expense splitting. If you need to split a meal between two clients, you’ll have to do it on the web app.

## Who should pick what

If you’re a 10-person startup with a tight budget, Zoho Expense is the cheapest option that still gives you custom workflows. Just be prepared to spend a weekend setting up the GL mappings.

If you’re a 50-person agency with a lot of client meals, Pleo’s split feature will save you hours each month. The foreign-transaction fee is annoying, but the time savings offset it.

If you’re a 200-person company with a dedicated AP team, Ramp’s real-time controls and approval routing will reduce the number of exceptions your team has to handle.

If you’re a fintech or SaaS company that also pays vendors, Brex’s bill pay feature will let you consolidate tools. Just watch out for the chargeback fee.

If you’re a marketing team with variable ad spend, Divvy’s budget pacing will help you avoid overspending. The ACH reversal fee is steep, but if you’re careful with submissions, you’ll rarely trigger it.

The next update to watch is Ramp’s planned offline mode for the mobile app. If they deliver it, that will remove the last major friction for teams that travel frequently. Until then, Pleo remains the best choice for road warriors.
