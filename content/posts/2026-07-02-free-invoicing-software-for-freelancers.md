Freelancers who start with free invoicing software usually hit the same wall: the free plan either runs out of invoices too fast or hides the good features behind a paywall. I’ve set up four different tools for solo designers, developers, and consultants over the past year. Here’s what you’ll run into.

## What you get for free (and what you don’t)

Most free plans let you send between 3 and 5 invoices a month. Wave and Zoho Invoice cap at 5, while Invoice Ninja gives you 20—but only if you host it yourself. If you’re not comfortable running a server, you’re back to 5.

Recurring invoices are usually free, but automated reminders often aren’t. Wave includes one reminder per invoice; Zoho charges after the first three. PayPal Invoicing doesn’t have reminders at all unless you upgrade.

Payment processing fees are the same no matter the plan: 2.9% + $0.30 for cards, 1% for bank transfers. The only way to lower these is to switch processors, which means leaving the invoicing tool behind.

## The tools I’ve tested with real freelancers

### Wave
Wave’s free plan covers unlimited invoices, clients, and expense tracking. The catch is that you can’t remove the Wave branding from invoices unless you pay $16 a month. Most freelancers I know don’t care about the logo, but clients sometimes ask why it’s there.

The mobile app is basic. You can create and send invoices, but you can’t reconcile bank transactions on your phone. If you’re on the road a lot, this becomes annoying fast.

### Zoho Invoice
Zoho’s free plan is limited to 1,000 invoices a year, which sounds like a lot until you realize it’s only 83 a month. If you invoice weekly, you’ll hit the cap in three months. The $9 plan removes the limit and adds time tracking, which is useful if you bill hourly.

The interface is clean, but the customization options are buried. You can change invoice templates, but you have to dig through three menus to find the settings. I’ve seen freelancers give up and stick with the default look.

### Invoice Ninja
Invoice Ninja’s free plan is the most generous on paper: 20 invoices a month, unlimited clients, and no branding. The catch is that you have to self-host. If you’re not technical, the hosted version starts at $10 a month.

The hosted version includes a client portal where customers can view and pay invoices. Wave and Zoho charge extra for this. If you want clients to have a self-service option, Invoice Ninja is the only free tool that offers it.

### PayPal Invoicing
PayPal’s invoicing is free and simple. You can send unlimited invoices, but there’s no expense tracking or reporting. If you only need to send a few invoices a month and already use PayPal for payments, it’s the easiest option.

The downside is that PayPal holds funds for new accounts or large transactions. I’ve had two freelancers get stuck waiting 21 days for a $2,000 payment because PayPal flagged it as “unusual activity.” Neither had any issues with other processors.

## Where each tool falls short

Wave’s reporting is weak. You can see basic income and expenses, but there’s no way to filter by client or project. If you need to pull numbers for taxes or a business review, you’ll end up exporting to a spreadsheet.

Zoho’s mobile app has offline mode, but it’s buggy. I’ve had invoices disappear when the app synced after being offline for a few hours. The desktop version is solid, so if you only work from one place, it’s not a problem.

Invoice Ninja’s self-hosted setup is a hassle. You need a server, SSL certificate, and regular backups. If you’re not comfortable with command-line tools, the hosted version is easier—but then you’re paying $10 a month.

PayPal’s customer service is slow. If a payment gets stuck or a client disputes an invoice, you’re looking at a 48-hour response time. Other tools usually reply within 24 hours.

## Who each tool is for

Wave is best if you want a free plan that doesn’t expire and you don’t mind the branding. It’s the only tool that includes expense tracking for free, which is useful if you’re tracking deductions.

Zoho Invoice works if you invoice less than 83 times a month and need time tracking. The $9 plan is worth it once you hit the free limit.

Invoice Ninja is the only free option with a client portal, but only if you self-host. If you’re not technical, the $10 plan is still cheaper than Wave’s $16.

PayPal Invoicing is the simplest option if you already use PayPal and don’t need reporting. It’s not a full accounting tool, but it gets invoices out the door fast.

## The one thing no one tells you

Most freelancers outgrow free plans within six months. The real cost isn’t the monthly fee—it’s the time you spend migrating data when you switch. Exporting invoices from Wave to Zoho took one designer two hours because the CSV format didn’t match. If you think you’ll need a paid plan soon, start with the tool you’ll upgrade to later.

If you’re just starting and only need to send a few invoices a month, PayPal or Wave will work. Once you hit 10 invoices a month, Zoho or Invoice Ninja’s paid plans are worth the cost. Pick the one that matches how you work now, not the one you hope to need later.
