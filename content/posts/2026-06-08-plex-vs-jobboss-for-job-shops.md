Plex won’t give you an on-premise option—even if your shop floor loses internet for hours every week. JobBOSS still runs on a 20-year-old interface that makes your team groan every time they open a work order. Neither is perfect, but one will fit your workflow better than the other.

## What You’ll Actually Pay

Plex’s pricing starts at $150 per user/month, but that’s just the base. You’ll pay extra for modules like quality management ($50/user/month) and advanced scheduling ($30/user/month). JobBOSS, meanwhile, charges a one-time fee of $10,000–$50,000 depending on user count and modules, plus 20% annual maintenance. For a 10-person shop, Plex runs about $23,000/year after add-ons, while JobBOSS costs $12,000 upfront and $2,400/year in maintenance.

Here’s how the costs break down over three years:

| Factor               | Plex (Cloud)          | JobBOSS (On-Premise)  |
|----------------------|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| Upfront Cost         | $0                    | $10,000–$50,000       |
| Annual Cost (Year 1) | ~$23,000              | $2,400 (maintenance)  |
| Annual Cost (Year 3) | ~$23,000              | $2,400                |
| Total 3-Year Cost    | ~$69,000              | $17,200–$57,200       |
| Internet Dependency  | Required              | None                  |
| Scalability          | Easy (add users)      | Limited (server cap)  |

JobBOSS looks cheaper on paper, but if you need to scale beyond 20 users, you’ll hit hardware limits fast. Plex scales smoothly—but only if you’re okay with recurring costs.

## Features That Actually Matter

### Scheduling: The Make-or-Break for Job Shops
Plex’s scheduling tool is visual and drag-and-drop, which is great until you realize it doesn’t account for machine downtime or operator skill levels. JobBOSS’s scheduler is clunky but lets you lock in constraints like “only run this job on the Haas with the 4th-axis.” If your shop runs high-mix, low-volume work, JobBOSS’s rigidity might actually save you from missed deadlines.

### Shop Floor Data Collection
Plex’s mobile app works offline and syncs when back online—critical for shops with spotty Wi-Fi. JobBOSS’s mobile app is a web wrapper that crashes if the connection drops. But JobBOSS lets you customize data collection fields without a developer, while Plex charges $200/hour for API tweaks.

### Inventory: Where JobBOSS Falls Behind
JobBOSS tracks inventory in real time, but its barcoding system requires manual scans for every transaction. Plex integrates with RFID and scales for high-volume shops. If you’re doing 100+ transactions a day, JobBOSS will slow you down.

## The Rough Edges

Plex’s biggest limitation? **No on-premise option.** If your shop is in a rural area with unreliable internet, you’re stuck. JobBOSS’s biggest limitation? **The UI looks like it’s from 2005.** New hires take weeks to train, and even veterans complain about the workflow.

Here’s what users actually say (from G2, as of May 2026):
- Plex: 4.2/5 (120+ reviews) – “Cloud dependency is a dealbreaker for us.” / “Scheduling is powerful but overkill for simple jobs.”
- JobBOSS: 3.8/5 (80+ reviews) – “Works, but the interface is painful.” / “Great for small shops, but don’t expect modern UX.”

## What the Vendor Websites Won’t Tell You

JobBOSS’s “unlimited users” claim is misleading. The software caps at 50 concurrent users, and performance tanks after 30. Plex’s “real-time analytics” require a separate Power BI license ($10/user/month) for full functionality.

On forums like Practical Machinist, users report that migrating from JobBOSS to Plex takes 6–9 months, not the 3 months Plex’s sales team promises. The biggest friction? Data mapping—JobBOSS’s legacy database structure doesn’t play nice with modern APIs.

## Which One Fits Your Shop?

If you’re a **10–20 person shop with stable internet and a need for scalability**, Plex is the better long-term bet. The recurring cost stings, but you’ll save on IT overhead.

If you’re a **small shop with unreliable internet, a tight budget, and simple workflows**, JobBOSS gets the job done. Just don’t expect to love the experience.

Neither is perfect. Pick the one that solves your biggest headache today.
