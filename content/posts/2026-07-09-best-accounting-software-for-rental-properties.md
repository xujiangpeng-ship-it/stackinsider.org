Most accounting software treats rental properties like a side hustle. You end up juggling spreadsheets for security deposits, chasing down late rent with generic invoices, and manually splitting utilities. The tools that do handle rentals often feel like overkill for small landlords or lack basic features for larger portfolios. Here’s what I’ve seen work (and what doesn’t) after setting up systems for 50+ property managers.

## What landlords actually need

Rental accounting isn’t just about tracking income and expenses. You need:

- Tenant-level ledgers that show rent payments, late fees, and security deposits separately.
- Automatic rent reminders tied to lease dates, not just recurring invoices.
- The ability to split bills (like utilities) across multiple units without exporting to Excel.
- Bank feeds that categorize transactions correctly the first time. Most tools default to "Repairs" for everything.

If you’re managing fewer than 10 units, you might get away with QuickBooks Online or Xero. But once you hit 20+ units, the workarounds add up. Landlords with 50+ units usually need a dedicated tool like AppFolio or Buildium.

## What you’ll actually pay

QuickBooks Online and Xero advertise low entry prices, but landlords often need add-ons. QuickBooks’ "Advanced" plan ($200/month) is the first tier with batch invoicing, which you’ll want for 10+ units. Xero’s $65/month plan includes payroll, but only for one state.

Rentec Direct’s $45/month plan covers 10 units. Each additional unit costs $1. If you have 25 units, that’s $60/month. The catch: you’ll need to pay extra for ACH payments ($0.50 per transaction) or credit card fees (2.9% + $0.30).

AppFolio’s pricing is straightforward but expensive. The $280 base fee includes 50 units. If you have 100 units, you’ll pay $350/month. Buildium’s $55/month plan covers 150 units, but you’ll pay $0.50 per unit for online payments.

Hidden costs add up. Most tools charge for:
- ACH or credit card payments (usually 1-3% per transaction).
- Background checks ($15-$30 per tenant).
- E-signatures ($10-$20 per document).
- Bank feeds (Xero charges $6/month for Yodlee).

## Where each tool shines (and where it doesn’t)

### QuickBooks Online
QuickBooks is the default for small landlords because it’s familiar. You can set up classes to track income and expenses by property, and the bank feeds work well. The mobile app lets you snap receipts, which is handy for repairs.

But QuickBooks wasn’t built for rentals. You’ll need to manually create invoices for rent each month, and there’s no built-in way to track security deposits. Landlords often use a separate liability account, which means extra steps to reconcile. The reporting is flexible, but you’ll spend time customizing templates for rental-specific metrics like vacancy rates or late fees.

### Xero
Xero is cheaper than QuickBooks and handles multi-currency well, which is useful if you own properties abroad. The bank reconciliation is faster, and you can set up repeating invoices for rent.

The downside: Xero’s inventory features are overkill for landlords, and the interface feels cluttered. You’ll need to use tracking categories for properties, which works but isn’t as intuitive as QuickBooks’ classes. The mobile app lacks some features, like editing bank rules.

### Rentec Direct
Rentec Direct is built for landlords, not accountants. You get tenant portals, online rent payments, and maintenance request tracking. The reporting includes vacancy rates and rent roll summaries out of the box.

The rough edges: The interface looks outdated, and the workflows feel rigid. You can’t customize invoice templates much, and the bank feeds sometimes miscategorize transactions. Landlords with 50+ units often complain about slow load times.

### AppFolio
AppFolio is the most feature-rich option. You get built-in tenant screening, lease templates, and a resident portal. The accounting is solid, with automatic late fees and security deposit tracking. The reporting includes everything from rent rolls to CAM reconciliations.

But AppFolio is expensive and complex. The learning curve is steep, and you’ll need training to use it effectively. The mobile app is limited, and some users report slow customer support. Landlords with fewer than 50 units often find it overwhelming.

### Buildium
Buildium sits between Rentec Direct and AppFolio. It’s more affordable than AppFolio but more polished than Rentec Direct. You get tenant portals, online payments, and maintenance tracking. The accounting includes automatic rent posting and security deposit management.

The limitations: Buildium’s reporting isn’t as flexible as QuickBooks or Xero. You can’t customize reports much, and the bank feeds sometimes require manual adjustments. The mobile app lacks some features, like editing lease terms.

## The workflows that trip landlords up

Most landlords start with QuickBooks or Xero because they already use it for other business. But rental accounting has unique needs that generic tools don’t handle well.

**Security deposits:** QuickBooks and Xero treat security deposits as income, which is wrong. You’ll need to manually move them to a liability account. Rentec Direct, AppFolio, and Buildium track them correctly out of the box.

**Late fees:** QuickBooks and Xero can’t automatically apply late fees based on lease terms. You’ll need to calculate them manually or use a third-party app. AppFolio and Buildium automate this.

**Utility splitting:** If you split utilities across units, you’ll need to export data to Excel with QuickBooks and Xero. Rentec Direct and Buildium let you split bills within the app.

**Bank feeds:** QuickBooks and Xero often miscategorize transactions. You’ll spend time fixing "Repairs" that should be "Maintenance" or "Utilities." AppFolio and Buildium are better at learning your categories over time.

## What users complain about

G2 reviews as of June 2026 show common frustrations:

- **QuickBooks Online:** Users with 20+ units say the reporting gets messy. "I spend more time organizing data than analyzing it," one reviewer wrote.
- **Xero:** Landlords complain about the lack of rental-specific features. "I had to build my own templates for rent rolls," another noted.
- **Rentec Direct:** Users report slow load times and a clunky interface. "It feels like software from 2010," one said.
- **AppFolio:** The steep learning curve and high cost are common complaints. "I needed a consultant to set it up," a user wrote.
- **Buildium:** Landlords with 100+ units say the reporting isn’t flexible enough. "I can’t get the data I need without exporting to Excel," one reviewer noted.

## Who should use what

If you manage 1-10 units and want a simple, familiar tool, QuickBooks Online is fine. You’ll need to work around its limitations, but the bank feeds and reporting are solid.

For 10-50 units, Rentec Direct or Buildium are better fits. Rentec Direct is cheaper but feels outdated. Buildium is more polished but lacks some customization.

If you have 50+ units and need advanced features like tenant screening and lease templates, AppFolio is the best option. The cost and complexity are worth it for larger portfolios.

Avoid Xero unless you’re already using it for other business. The rental features are lacking, and you’ll end up doing more manual work.
