Free accounting software sounds great until you hit the first invoice limit or realise the bank feed costs extra. I’ve set up five of these tools for UK clients in the last twelve months—sole traders, e-commerce start-ups, and a charity that needed VAT handling without the £30-a-month bill. Here’s what you’ll run into, which tools still make sense in 2026, and which ones to skip.

## What “free” really means in 2026

Most vendors use “free” to mean “free for the first 30 days” or “free if you only send three invoices a month.” The four tools that actually stay free after sign-up are Wave, FreeAgent (for NatWest/RBS customers), Zoho Books (free tier), and Pandle. Each one caps something:

- Wave: unlimited invoices, but no UK-specific VAT return filing.
- FreeAgent: full VAT filing, but only if you bank with NatWest Group.
- Zoho Books: 1,000 invoices a year, no bank feeds without a paid add-on.
- Pandle: unlimited invoices, but the mobile app is read-only.

If you’re VAT-registered and outside NatWest, only Pandle and FreeAgent let you file directly to HMRC without upgrading. Wave forces you to export a CSV and upload manually—fine once a quarter, annoying every month.

## Where each tool shines (and where it doesn’t)

Wave
The interface is simple, and the free tier is genuinely unlimited. You can send 500 invoices a month and still not pay a penny. The catch: no UK VAT filing automation. Wave’s Canadian roots mean the VAT report is a generic sales-tax summary. You’ll spend 20 minutes each quarter manually mapping boxes 1–9. If you’re not VAT-registered, this is the easiest free option.

FreeAgent
If you bank with NatWest, RBS, or Ulster Bank, FreeAgent is effectively free forever. It handles Construction Industry Scheme (CIS) deductions, files VAT directly to HMRC, and even reminds you when a client’s payment is late. The mobile app lets you snap receipts and auto-match them to bank transactions. Outside those banks, the £19/month price jumps to £29 if you want payroll, so it’s no longer free.

Zoho Books
The free tier is tight—1,000 invoices a year, one user, no bank feeds. You can connect a bank account, but Zoho charges £5 a month for the feed. The real value is in the ecosystem: if you already use Zoho CRM or Inventory, the free accounting tier syncs contacts and stock levels. For everyone else, the cap makes it impractical once you hit 20 invoices a week.

Pandle
Pandle is the only free tool that files VAT and lets you reconcile bank transactions without upgrading. The mobile app is read-only, so you can’t create invoices on the go, but the desktop version is fast and the reporting is HMRC-compliant. Payroll is £5 a month plus £1 per employee—half the cost of FreeAgent. The downside: no time tracking or project management, so agencies and freelancers will feel the gap.

## The rough edges you’ll hit

Bank feeds break
All four tools rely on open banking. If your bank changes its API (Monzo did this in March 2026), the feed stops until the accounting software updates its integration. Wave and Pandle usually fix it within a week; Zoho can take a month. Keep a CSV export habit.

VAT filing quirks
FreeAgent and Pandle file directly, but neither handles Flat Rate Scheme calculations automatically. You’ll still need to work out the 16.5 % rate yourself if you’re on the limited-cost trader band.

Mobile limitations
Only Wave and FreeAgent let you create and send invoices from a phone. Pandle’s mobile app is read-only, and Zoho’s free tier doesn’t include mobile access at all.

Payroll is never free
Every tool charges extra for payroll. Pandle is the cheapest at £5 + £1 per employee, but it doesn’t file Real Time Information (RTI) to HMRC—you have to do that manually. FreeAgent includes RTI filing in its payroll add-on, which is why it costs more.

## Who each tool is for

- Sole trader, not VAT-registered, wants unlimited invoices: Wave.
- NatWest/RBS customer, needs VAT filing and payroll: FreeAgent.
- Existing Zoho user, low invoice volume, happy to pay £5 for bank feeds: Zoho Books.
- Small business, VAT-registered, wants the cheapest payroll: Pandle.

If you’re a limited company with employees, none of these stay free forever. The moment you add payroll, you’re looking at £15–£30 a month. At that point, it’s worth comparing the paid tiers of FreeAgent (£29) and QuickBooks (£25) to see which gives you more for the money.

## What to watch in the next 12 months

FreeAgent has started nudging non-NatWest users toward its paid tier by adding a “Pro” badge to new features. If you’re on the free plan, expect more prompts to upgrade. Zoho is testing a new “Zoho One Lite” bundle that includes accounting, CRM, and inventory for £20 a month—watch for that if you’re already in the Zoho ecosystem.

Pandle is the only vendor that hasn’t changed its free tier in two years. It’s stable, but don’t expect new features soon.

## Final call

If you’re a sole trader who just needs to send invoices and track expenses, Wave is the simplest free option. If you’re VAT-registered and bank with NatWest, FreeAgent is the clear winner. For everyone else, Pandle gives you VAT filing and bank feeds without the monthly fee, even if the mobile app is basic.

Pick the one that matches your bank and your invoice volume today. You can always export your data and switch later—none of these lock you in.
