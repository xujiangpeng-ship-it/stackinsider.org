Agile CRM’s free tier is a trap. It caps you at 1,000 contacts and 1 user, but the moment you invite a second team member, you’re forced into the $8.99/user/month Starter plan—even if you don’t need the extra features. Bitrix24, on the other hand, lets you add up to 5 users for free, with no contact limits. That’s a dealbreaker for small teams testing the waters.

## What You’ll Actually Pay

Agile CRM’s pricing looks simple at first glance, but the tiers are rigid. The Starter plan ($8.99/user/month) includes basic automation and reporting, but if you want custom deal tracks or advanced telephony, you’re jumping to the Regular plan at $29.99/user/month. Bitrix24’s pricing is more modular—you can buy just the CRM, just the project management, or the full suite. Their CRM-only plan starts at $49/month for 5 users, which is cheaper per seat than Agile CRM’s Regular tier if you have a small team.

Here’s how the costs break down for a 5-person team over a year:

| Feature                     | Agile CRM (Regular) | Bitrix24 (CRM+)      |
|-----------------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Monthly cost                | $149.95             | $99                  |
| Annual cost                 | $1,799.40           | $1,188               |
| Contact limit               | 50,000              | Unlimited            |
| Custom deal tracks          | Yes                 | Yes                  |
| Telephony                   | Yes                 | Add-on ($24/month)   |
| Project management          | No                  | Yes                  |

Bitrix24’s telephony is an add-on, but Agile CRM’s built-in calling feature is clunky. Users report dropped calls and poor audio quality, and the mobile app doesn’t support call recording at all. If phone support is critical, you’ll need a third-party integration like Aircall or JustCall with either tool.

## Where It Shines (and Where It Doesn’t)

Agile CRM’s automation is its strongest suit. You can set up complex workflows with branching logic—like sending a follow-up email only if a lead hasn’t responded in 3 days, or assigning a task to a sales rep when a deal moves to a new stage. Bitrix24’s automation is simpler, with pre-built templates that work well for basic sequences but fall short for nuanced processes. For example, Agile CRM lets you trigger automations based on custom fields, while Bitrix24 limits you to standard fields unless you’re on the Enterprise plan.

Bitrix24, however, wins on collaboration. Its built-in project management tools include Kanban boards, Gantt charts, and time tracking—features Agile CRM lacks entirely. If your team needs to manage client projects alongside sales pipelines, Bitrix24 is the clear choice. Agile CRM forces you to integrate with a separate tool like Trello or Asana, adding friction and cost.

The mobile apps tell a different story. Agile CRM’s app is barebones, with no offline mode and limited filtering options. Bitrix24’s app is more polished, but it’s slow to load and crashes frequently on Android devices. Neither is great, but Bitrix24 at least lets you access project tasks and CRM data in one place.

## The Rough Edges

Agile CRM’s reporting is a mess. The pre-built dashboards are cluttered, and custom reports require manual SQL queries if you need anything beyond basic metrics. Bitrix24’s reporting is more intuitive, but it lacks advanced filters for date ranges or custom fields unless you’re on the Enterprise plan.

Integration friction is another pain point. Agile CRM’s Zapier integration is reliable, but native integrations with tools like QuickBooks or Xero are outdated. Bitrix24’s marketplace has more options, but users report that some apps (like the Google Drive integration) break after updates. Neither tool plays nicely with legacy systems—if you’re migrating from an old CRM, expect manual data cleaning.

Bitrix24’s biggest limitation is its learning curve. The interface is packed with features, but the navigation is unintuitive. New users often struggle to find basic functions, like editing a deal or setting up a recurring task. Agile CRM’s interface is cleaner, but the lack of project management tools makes it feel incomplete for teams that need more than sales tracking.

## What Users Actually Say

On G2, Agile CRM holds a 4.1/5 rating (as of June 2026), with users praising its automation but criticizing the outdated UI and poor customer support. Bitrix24 scores slightly higher at 4.2/5, but the complaints are louder: slow performance, confusing pricing, and a steep onboarding process. One user on Reddit noted, “Bitrix24 is like a Swiss Army knife—it does everything, but none of it particularly well.”

A hidden cost with Bitrix24 is storage. The free plan includes only 5GB, and the CRM+ plan jumps to 100GB, but if you’re storing large files (like video recordings or design assets), you’ll hit that limit fast. Agile CRM doesn’t cap storage, but the lack of project management means you’ll need another tool for file sharing anyway.

## Which One Should You Choose?

If you’re a small sales team focused on automation and don’t need project management, Agile CRM is the better pick—just be prepared to outgrow it quickly. For teams that need CRM *and* project tools in one place, Bitrix24 is the more flexible option, but budget for training and expect some frustration with the interface.

The real question isn’t which tool is “better”—it’s which one fits your workflow. Agile CRM forces you to adapt to its limitations, while Bitrix24 gives you more tools than you might need. Neither is perfect, but one will fit your team’s rhythm. Choose accordingly.
