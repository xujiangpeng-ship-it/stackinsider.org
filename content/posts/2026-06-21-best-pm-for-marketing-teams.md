**ClickUp’s killer feature for marketers:** Custom task statuses. You’re not stuck with “To Do/In Progress/Done.” Need “Awaiting Legal Review” or “Client Feedback Pending”? ClickUp lets you build it. This alone saved one team I worked with 10+ hours a month in status update meetings.

But here’s the catch: ClickUp’s flexibility is a double-edged sword. The same team that loved custom statuses spent two weeks over-engineering their workspace with nested folders, custom fields, and automations that no one used. If your team isn’t disciplined, ClickUp becomes a cluttered mess. The mobile app is also notoriously slow—don’t expect to approve a social post on the go.

**Asana’s strength:** Simplicity. It’s the only tool here that doesn’t feel like it’s trying to be a CRM, a chat app, and a PM tool all at once. For teams that just need to track campaigns and deadlines without the bells and whistles, Asana is the least frustrating option. The timeline view (Gantt chart) is the cleanest of the three, and the “Portfolios” feature lets you roll up multiple projects into a high-level view—great for reporting to stakeholders.

The trade-off? Asana’s marketing-specific features are weak. No native proofing, no asset management, and the free plan caps you at 15 users. If you’re a small team with basic needs, it’s fine. If you’re scaling, you’ll hit walls fast.

**Monday.com’s edge:** Visual appeal. The color-coded boards are easy to scan, and the “Workdocs” feature lets you embed live data from your boards into documents—useful for campaign briefs. Monday also has the best native integrations for marketing tools (HubSpot, Mailchimp, etc.), though most require the Pro plan.

But Monday’s pricing is sneaky. The “Standard” plan ($10/user/month) is the minimum viable tier for most teams, but it doesn’t include time tracking, automations, or guest access. By the time you add those, you’re closer to $20/user/month. Also, Monday’s automations are powerful but confusing to set up. One client’s team spent a full sprint just building workflows for their content calendar.

### The Dark Horse: Airtable
If your marketing team lives in spreadsheets but needs more structure, Airtable is worth a look. It’s not a traditional PM tool, but its grid/database hybrid is perfect for content calendars, campaign tracking, and asset libraries. The “Interfaces” feature lets you build custom dashboards for different stakeholders (e.g., a high-level view for executives, a detailed one for creatives).

**Real-world win:** A 70-person marketing team used Airtable to consolidate their content calendar, social media scheduler, and asset library into one tool. They cut their tool stack from five apps to two (Airtable + a social scheduler).

**Big limitation:** Airtable isn’t great for task dependencies or complex project timelines. If your workflows involve “Task B can’t start until Task A is approved,” you’ll need to jury-rig it with automations or look elsewhere.

## The Rough Edges

### What Vendors Won’t Tell You
1. **Migrations are messy.** Moving from spreadsheets or another PM tool isn’t just about exporting/importing data. One team I worked with spent three weeks cleaning up their Asana workspace after importing from Trello because the due dates and assignees didn’t map correctly. ClickUp’s migration tool is better, but it still doesn’t handle custom fields well.

2. **Integrations aren’t plug-and-play.** Monday.com’s HubSpot integration, for example, only syncs contacts and deals—not custom objects. If you’re using HubSpot for campaign tracking, you’ll need to build a workaround. Similarly, Asana’s Slack integration is great for notifications but terrible for updating tasks from Slack. Expect to spend time tweaking.

3. **Offline mode is a myth.** None of these tools have a true offline mode. ClickUp’s mobile app lets you view tasks offline, but you can’t edit them. If your team works in areas with spotty Wi-Fi (e.g., events, trade shows), this is a real problem.

4. **Proofing tools are overrated.** ClickUp and Monday.com both offer native proofing, but in practice, teams still use Figma or Adobe Creative Cloud for feedback. The native tools work for simple image reviews but fall short for video or complex designs.

### The One Thing No One Talks About
**Adoption is the real hurdle.** The best PM tool in the world is useless if your team won’t use it. I’ve seen teams abandon ClickUp because it was too complex, Asana because it was too simple, and Monday.com because the boards felt “too corporate.” The fix? Involve your team in the selection process. Run a two-week trial with a small group, then ask for brutal feedback. If the creatives hate it, no amount of automations will save you.

## What You’ll Actually Pay

Here’s the pricing breakdown as of June 2026, straight from the vendors’ sites:

- **ClickUp:** Free plan (limited features), $12/user/month (Unlimited), $19/user/month (Business). The Business plan is the sweet spot for marketing teams—it includes proofing, custom roles, and advanced automations. Watch out for the “per member” pricing for guests (e.g., clients or freelancers). If you have 10 team members and 5 guests, you’re paying for 15 seats.

- **Asana:** $10.99/user/month (Premium), $24.99/user/month (Business). The Premium plan is fine for small teams, but the Business plan is where you get approvals, proofing, and advanced reporting. Asana’s pricing is straightforward, but the jump from Premium to Business is steep.

- **Monday.com:** $8/user/month (Basic), $10/user/month (Standard), $16/user/month (Pro). The Standard plan is the minimum viable tier, but it doesn’t include time tracking or automations. The Pro plan is where you get guest access, private boards, and advanced integrations. Monday’s pricing is the most confusing—make sure to calculate the true cost before committing.

- **Airtable:** Free plan (limited records), $10/user/month (Plus), $20/user/month (Pro). The Pro plan is where you get advanced interfaces and automations. Airtable’s pricing is per “workspace editor,” not per user, which can get expensive if you have a lot of collaborators.

**Hidden cost alert:** All these tools charge extra for premium support. ClickUp’s Business plan includes “priority support,” but if you’re on the Unlimited plan, you’re stuck with email-only responses. Monday.com’s Pro plan includes “24/7 priority support,” but good luck getting a human on the phone.

## Where It Falls Short

### ClickUp
- **Overwhelming for new users.** The learning curve is steep. One team I worked with had to hire a ClickUp consultant to set up their workspace because the built-in templates were too generic.
- **Mobile app is slow.** The iOS app takes 5–10 seconds to load tasks, which is a dealbreaker for teams that need to update statuses on the go.
- **Automations are powerful but buggy.** I’ve seen automations fail silently, especially when dealing with custom fields.

### Asana
- **No native proofing.** You’ll need to integrate with a third-party tool like Filestage or Ziflow, which adds cost and complexity.
- **Limited customization.** If you need custom fields or statuses, you’re out of luck unless you’re on the Business plan.
- **No offline mode.** Even the mobile app requires an internet connection.

### Monday.com
- **Pricing is opaque.** The “per user” pricing doesn’t account for guests or viewers, which can inflate costs quickly.
- **Automations are hard to set up.** The visual builder is intuitive, but the logic behind it isn’t. Expect to spend time troubleshooting.
- **No native time tracking in lower tiers.** If you need to track time spent on tasks, you’ll need the Pro plan or a third-party integration.

### Airtable
- **Not a true PM tool.** If you need Gantt charts or task dependencies, you’ll need to build them yourself.
- **Limited reporting.** The built-in reporting tools are basic. If you need advanced analytics, you’ll need to export data to another tool.
- **Pricing scales poorly.** The more records and collaborators you have, the more expensive it gets.

## The Verdict

Here’s who each tool is best for:

- **ClickUp:** Marketing teams that need flexibility and don’t mind a steep learning curve. Best for teams of 10+ that juggle complex campaigns, content calendars, and creative workflows. Avoid if your team is small or resistant to change.

- **Asana:** Teams that want simplicity and don’t need deep marketing-specific features. Best for small to mid-sized teams (5–50 people) that need to track deadlines and collaborate without friction. Avoid if you need proofing or advanced automations.

- **Monday.com:** Teams that prioritize visual appeal and native integrations. Best for mid-sized teams (20–100 people) that use a lot of marketing tools (HubSpot, Mailchimp, etc.). Avoid if you’re on a tight budget or need offline access.

- **Airtable:** Teams that live in spreadsheets but need more structure. Best for content teams, social media managers, and asset libraries. Avoid if you need true project management features like task dependencies.

If you’re still unsure, run a two-week trial with your top two choices. Give your team a real project to manage in each tool, then ask for feedback. The tool that feels the least frustrating after two weeks is the one you should pick.

And if you’re migrating from another tool, budget at least a month for the transition. The last thing you want is to pay for two tools while your team struggles to adapt.
