Here’s the first gotcha: neither BambooHR nor Gusto publishes their full pricing online. You’ll need to book a sales call to get a quote—and that’s where the surprises start. Teams often assume they’re comparing apples to apples, but the pricing models are fundamentally different. BambooHR charges per employee, per month, with add-ons that can double your bill. Gusto bundles payroll into its base price, but only if you’re in one of the 50 U.S. states it supports. If you’re outside the U.S. or need global payroll, Gusto isn’t even an option.

## What You’ll Actually Pay

BambooHR’s pricing starts at $6.19 per employee, per month—but that’s for the *Essentials* plan, which doesn’t include time tracking, performance management, or onboarding. The *Advantage* plan, which most teams need, starts at $8.25 per employee, per month. Add-ons like performance management ($4.95/employee/month) or time tracking ($3/employee/month) push the total closer to $15–$20 per employee. For a 50-person team, that’s $750–$1,000 per month before taxes or implementation fees.

Gusto’s *Simple* plan starts at $40/month + $6 per employee. For the same 50-person team, that’s $340/month—less than half of BambooHR’s base price. But here’s the catch: Gusto’s *Simple* plan only includes payroll and basic HR. To get time tracking, benefits administration, or performance tools, you’ll need the *Plus* plan ($80/month + $12 per employee), bringing the total to $680/month. Still cheaper than BambooHR, but the gap narrows.

**Real-world data point:** According to a [G2 review from May 2024](https://www.g2.com/products/gusto/reviews), 28% of Gusto users reported unexpected fees, primarily from state payroll taxes or third-party integrations. BambooHR users, meanwhile, frequently cite add-on costs as a frustration—especially for performance management, which is often a must-have for growing teams.

## Where It Shines (and Where It Doesn’t)

BambooHR’s strength is its flexibility. If you don’t need payroll, it’s a cleaner HRIS with better reporting and customization. The onboarding workflows are a standout: new hires can complete paperwork, watch training videos, and even sign documents before their first day. For remote teams, this is a game-changer—no more chasing signatures or lost PDFs.

Gusto, on the other hand, is built for small businesses that want payroll and HR in one place. The payroll processing is seamless: direct deposit, tax filings, and even contractor payments are handled automatically. But if you’re outside the U.S., you’re out of luck. Even U.S.-based teams with international contractors hit a wall—Gusto doesn’t support global payroll, so you’ll need a separate tool like Deel or Remote.

**Hidden limitation:** Gusto’s time tracking is barebones. Employees can clock in/out, but there’s no GPS verification or project-based tracking. BambooHR’s time tracking integrates with its performance module, so managers can see how time spent correlates with goals. If time tracking is critical for your team (e.g., agencies or field workers), BambooHR is the better pick—even at a higher cost.

## The Rough Edges

BambooHR’s mobile app is slow. Not “could be faster” slow—*painfully* slow. Employees report lag when submitting time-off requests or checking pay stubs, and the app lacks offline mode. For teams that rely on mobile access (retail, healthcare, or field workers), this is a dealbreaker.

Gusto’s customer support is hit or miss. The *Plus* plan includes phone support, but users on the *Simple* plan are limited to email and chat. Response times vary—some users report resolutions in hours, while others wait days. BambooHR’s support is more consistent, with a dedicated account manager for teams over 50 employees.

**Community sentiment:** On Reddit’s r/HRProfessionals, Gusto users praise its ease of use but complain about limited customization. BambooHR users love the reporting but gripe about the pricing structure. One user put it bluntly: *“BambooHR is like a Swiss Army knife—expensive, but you’ll use all the tools. Gusto is a pocketknife—cheap and gets the job done, but don’t expect it to do everything.”*

## Which One Fits Your Team?

If you’re a U.S.-based small business (under 100 employees) that needs payroll + basic HR, Gusto is the clear winner on price. The bundled payroll and benefits administration save time and money, and the interface is intuitive enough for non-HR managers.

If you’re a growing team (50+ employees) with complex HR needs—performance reviews, advanced reporting, or global contractors—BambooHR is worth the premium. The add-ons add up, but the flexibility and integrations (like Slack, Greenhouse, or ADP) make it a better long-term fit.

For teams outside the U.S. or those needing global payroll, neither is ideal. Look at alternatives like Deel, Remote, or Rippling, which offer more international support.
