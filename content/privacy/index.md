## Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** June 15, 2026  
**Last Updated:** June 15, 2026

At **StackInsider** (https://stackinsider.org), we take your privacy seriously. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding your personal data. This policy complies with the **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** for EU/EEA users, the **California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)** for California residents, and **Google AdSense Program Policies**.

---

### 1. Information We Collect

#### 1.1 Information You Provide Voluntarily
We do not require user accounts or registration to access our content. However, if you contact us via email (e.g., editorial@stackinsider.org), we receive your email address and any information you choose to include in your message.

#### 1.2 Information Collected Automatically
When you visit StackInsider, certain information is automatically collected:

- **Log Data**: IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URL, pages visited, time and date of visit
- **Device Information**: Device type, screen resolution, language settings
- **Usage Data**: Page views, time on page, scroll depth, click patterns (via Google Analytics, if enabled)

#### 1.3 Cookies and Tracking Technologies
We use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

| Cookie Category | Purpose | Example |
|----------------|---------|---------|
| Essential | Site functionality (e.g., cookie consent preferences) | `cookie_consent` |
| Analytics | Understand how visitors use our site | Google Analytics cookies |
| Advertising | Serve relevant ads and measure ad performance | Google AdSense cookies |

### 2. Third-Party Services

#### 2.1 Google AdSense
StackInsider displays advertisements served by **Google AdSense**, a third-party advertising network. Google AdSense uses cookies and web beacons to serve ads based on your prior visits to this website and other websites.

- Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visit to StackInsider and/or other sites on the Internet
- These cookies do not contain personally identifiable information
- You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting [Google Ads Settings](https://adssettings.google.com)

#### 2.2 Google Analytics
We may use Google Analytics to understand site traffic and user behavior. Google Analytics collects anonymized data including:
- Pages visited and time on page
- Geographic location (country/city level only)
- Device and browser information

We have configured Google Analytics to:
- Anonymize IP addresses (`anonymizeIp: true`)
- Respect Do Not Track browser signals
- Retain data for 14 months

### 3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

- **Operate and improve our website**: Analyze content performance, fix technical issues, optimize page speed
- **Serve advertisements**: Google AdSense uses cookie data to display relevant ads
- **Respond to inquiries**: If you email us, we use your contact information to reply
- **Comply with legal obligations**: Respond to lawful requests from law enforcement

We do **not**:
- Sell personal data to third parties
- Use automated decision-making or profiling
- Collect sensitive personal data (health, biometrics, political opinions, etc.)

### 4. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR)

For users in the European Economic Area (EEA) and the United Kingdom, our legal basis for processing personal data is:

| Processing Activity | Legal Basis |
|--------------------|-------------|
| Essential cookies and site operation | Legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f)) |
| Analytics cookies | Consent (Article 6(1)(a)) |
| Advertising cookies | Consent (Article 6(1)(a)) |

You may withdraw consent at any time by adjusting your cookie preferences via the cookie consent banner.

### 5. Your Data Rights

#### 5.1 GDPR Rights (EEA/UK Users)
Under GDPR, you have the right to:
- **Access**: Request a copy of your personal data
- **Rectification**: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
- **Erasure**: Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
- **Restriction**: Limit how we process your data
- **Portability**: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
- **Objection**: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
- **Withdraw Consent**: Withdraw previously given consent at any time

#### 5.2 CCPA Rights (California Residents)
Under CCPA, California residents have the right to:
- **Know**: Request disclosure of categories and specific pieces of personal data collected
- **Delete**: Request deletion of personal data
- **Opt-out**: Opt out of the sale of personal data (we do not sell personal data)
- **Non-discrimination**: Not be discriminated against for exercising CCPA rights

#### 5.3 Exercising Your Rights
To exercise any of these rights, contact us at **privacy@stackinsider.org**. We will respond within:
- **GDPR**: 30 calendar days
- **CCPA**: 45 calendar days (with possible 45-day extension)

We may request verification of your identity before processing your request.

### 7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data:
- HTTPS encryption for all data transmission

- No storage of sensitive personal data

However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

StackInsider is not directed at children under 13 (or 16 in the EEA). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, contact us immediately.
