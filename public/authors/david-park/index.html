David Park is the senior editor at StackInsider, responsible for maintaining the publication’s editorial standards. Every review published on StackInsider passes through David’s desk for a final quality check — ensuring factual accuracy, structural clarity, and adherence to the site’s no-pay-to-play editorial policy.

Background

David brings 15+ years of technology publishing experience to StackInsider. Before joining, he served as managing editor at two major B2B tech publications, overseeing editorial teams of 10+ writers and managing content pipelines producing 50+ articles per month. He has edited over 5,000 technology articles spanning enterprise software, cloud computing, cybersecurity, and developer tools.

Expertise

  • Editorial Quality: Multi-stage review process ensuring every article meets professional publishing standards
  • Fact-Checking: Systematic verification of claims, pricing data, and feature comparisons against primary sources
  • Content Strategy: Developing editorial calendars, content formats, and review methodologies
  • Technical Clarity: Translating complex technical concepts into accessible, actionable prose

Editorial Standards

David developed StackInsider’s three-stage editorial review process: practitioner-written first drafts, peer review by a second domain expert, and final editorial polish. This process ensures that every review combines deep domain expertise with professional publishing quality.
